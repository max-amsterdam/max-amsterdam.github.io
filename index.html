Personal Projects

I am the co-founder and creator of c150data.com, a website designed for the Columbia Lightweight Rowing team (C150). The website stores real-time workout data for all C150 athletes, and gives meaning to the data by ranking athletes based on many factors, like training volume and intensity. In the winter of 2019, I spoke with my coach about what we could do to improve the efficiency of the team's training. We discovered that the key to creating an effective training plan is paying attention to how athletes repsond to training, which is only possible by examining the training of every athlete on the team. The platform that my coach analyzed training with is called TrainingPeaks. In a perfect world, all athletes would log all of their workouts in TrainingPeaks, and my coach would be able to easily compare athletes and training to adapt the training plan and help make selection. The problem with this system is two-fold: first, athletes are not motivated enough to log all their training. The benefits of consistently logging training only come after a long amount of time, so many athletes don't take the short amount of time it takes daily to log. Second, TrainingPeaks does not offer a lot of customizability to directly rank athletes. I decided to team up with a fellow rower and CS major to develop a website to fix these problems.
